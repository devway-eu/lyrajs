# Code of Conduct

## Our Pledge

We as members, contributors, and leaders pledge to make participation in the LyraJS community a harassment-free experience for everyone, regardless of age, body size, visible or invisible disability, ethnicity, sex characteristics, gender identity and expression, level of experience, education, socio-economic status, nationality, personal appearance, race, caste, color, religion, or sexual identity and orientation.

We pledge to act and interact in ways that contribute to an open, welcoming, diverse, inclusive, and healthy community.

## Our Standards

### Expected Behavior

Examples of behavior that contributes to a positive environment for our community include:

- **Being respectful and inclusive** - Treat everyone with respect and kindness
- **Using welcoming language** - Be friendly and patient with community members
- **Being collaborative** - Work together and help others learn
- **Accepting constructive feedback** - Be open to different viewpoints and experiences
- **Focusing on what is best for the community** - Put community interests first
- **Showing empathy** - Be understanding and considerate towards others
- **Giving credit** - Acknowledge contributions and give credit where due
- **Being patient with newcomers** - Remember everyone starts somewhere
- **Providing constructive criticism** - Offer helpful feedback when reviewing code
- **Respecting different skill levels** - Value contributions from all experience levels

### Unacceptable Behavior

Examples of unacceptable behavior include:

- **Harassment and discrimination** - Any form of harassment, discrimination, or prejudice
- **Trolling and insulting comments** - Deliberate intimidation, trolling, or derogatory remarks
- **Personal or political attacks** - Attacking someone personally or their political views
- **Public or private harassment** - Harassment in any form, public or private
- **Sexual content** - Sexualized language, imagery, or unwelcome sexual attention
- **Doxxing** - Publishing others' private information without permission
- **Spam and self-promotion** - Excessive unsolicited advertising or promotion
- **Plagiarism** - Copying others' work without proper attribution
- **Dishonesty** - Deliberately spreading misinformation or lying
- **Disruptive behavior** - Sustained disruption of discussions or events
- **Violating privacy** - Sharing private conversations without consent
- **Other conduct** - Other conduct which could reasonably be considered inappropriate in a professional setting

## Technical Conduct

### In Code Reviews

- Focus on the code, not the person
- Provide specific, actionable feedback
- Be constructive and supportive
- Assume good intentions
- Explain your reasoning clearly
- Accept that there may be different valid approaches

### In Discussions and Issues

- Search before posting to avoid duplicates
- Stay on topic and keep discussions focused
- Provide sufficient context and information
- Use appropriate channels and categories
- Be patient waiting for responses
- Don't ping maintainers unnecessarily
- Mark issues as resolved when answered

### In Contributions

- Follow the contributing guidelines ([CONTRIBUTING.md](./CONTRIBUTING.md))
- Write clear commit messages and PR descriptions
- Test your code before submitting
- Be responsive to review feedback
- Respect maintainers' decisions
- Don't submit malicious or intentionally broken code

## Scope

This Code of Conduct applies within all community spaces, including:

- GitHub repositories (code, issues, pull requests, discussions)
- Discord server (all channels)
- Official social media accounts
- Community events (virtual or in-person)
- Email communications
- Any other official LyraJS spaces

This Code of Conduct also applies when an individual is officially representing the community in public spaces. Examples include:

- Using an official LyraJS email address
- Posting via official social media accounts
- Acting as an appointed representative at events
- Speaking on behalf of the LyraJS project

## Enforcement Responsibilities

Community leaders are responsible for clarifying and enforcing our standards of acceptable behavior and will take appropriate and fair corrective action in response to any behavior that they deem inappropriate, threatening, offensive, or harmful.

Community leaders have the right and responsibility to remove, edit, or reject:
- Comments, commits, code, wiki edits, issues, and other contributions that are not aligned with this Code of Conduct
- Contributors temporarily or permanently for behaviors they deem inappropriate, threatening, offensive, or harmful

## Reporting Guidelines

### How to Report

If you experience or witness unacceptable behavior, or have any other concerns, please report it by contacting the project team:

**Primary Contact:**
- Email: matthieu@dev-way.eu
- Subject line: `[CoC] Brief description of issue`

**Alternative Contact:**
- GitHub: Open a confidential issue on the repository (if feature available)
- Discord: DM a @Maintainer or @Admin role member

### What to Include

When reporting, please include:

1. **Your contact information** - So we can follow up
2. **Names (usernames) of people involved** - Including witnesses
3. **Description of the incident** - What happened, when, where
4. **Supporting evidence** - Screenshots, links, logs (if available)
5. **Impact** - How it affected you or the community
6. **Any other relevant context** - Previous incidents, patterns, etc.

### Confidentiality

All reports will be handled with discretion and confidentiality. We will:

- Protect the privacy of the reporter
- Not share reporter identity without permission
- Keep investigation details confidential
- Only involve necessary parties in the resolution

## Enforcement Process

### 1. Investigation

Upon receiving a report, community leaders will:

1. Acknowledge receipt within 48 hours
2. Review the report and gather additional information
3. Consult with other community leaders if needed
4. Make a determination based on evidence

### 2. Determination

Community leaders will determine if a violation occurred and the severity:

- **Level 1:** Minor violation - Unintentional or first-time offense
- **Level 2:** Moderate violation - Repeated or more serious offense
- **Level 3:** Severe violation - Serious or repeated harmful behavior
- **Level 4:** Critical violation - Extreme or dangerous behavior

### 3. Consequences

Based on the severity, consequences may include:

#### Level 1: Warning
**Impact:** First-time minor violations

**Consequence:**
- Private written warning explaining the violation
- Clarification of expected behavior
- Request for apology or correction

#### Level 2: Temporary Ban
**Impact:** Repeated minor violations or moderate violation

**Consequence:**
- Temporary ban from community spaces (7-30 days)
- No interaction with community during ban period
- Violation of ban terms may result in permanent ban

#### Level 3: Permanent Ban
**Impact:** Severe violations or pattern of inappropriate behavior

**Consequence:**
- Permanent ban from all community spaces
- All contributions and access revoked
- Public announcement if necessary for community safety

#### Level 4: Immediate Permanent Ban
**Impact:** Critical violations (threats, doxxing, severe harassment)

**Consequence:**
- Immediate permanent ban without warning
- Possible legal action if warranted
- Public announcement to protect the community
- Report to platform authorities (GitHub, Discord, etc.)

### 4. Appeals

If you believe you were unfairly penalized:

1. You may appeal within 14 days of the decision
2. Send appeal to: matthieu@dev-way.eu with subject `[CoC Appeal]`
3. Include your perspective and any new information
4. Decision on appeals is final

## Enforcement Examples

### Example 1: Constructive Disagreement âœ…

**Scenario:** Two contributors disagree on an implementation approach

**Good Response:**
> "I understand your approach, but I'm concerned about the performance implications. Have you considered using [alternative] instead? Here's a benchmark showing the difference: [link]. What do you think?"

**Why it's good:** Respectful, specific, provides evidence, invites discussion

### Example 2: Unhelpful Criticism âŒ

**Scenario:** Reviewing a beginner's pull request

**Bad Response:**
> "This code is terrible. Did you even read the docs? Any experienced developer would know this is wrong."

**Better Response:**
> "Thanks for the PR! A few suggestions: 1) Consider using async/await instead of callbacks for better readability. Here's an example: [link]. 2) Our style guide prefers [X] over [Y]. Let me know if you need help!"

**Why it's better:** Constructive, specific, helpful, encouraging

### Example 3: Handling Frustration âœ…

**Scenario:** Frustrated with a bug after hours of debugging

**Good Response:**
> "I've been debugging this for hours and I'm stuck. Here's what I've tried: [list]. Could someone point me in the right direction? Code and error message: [link]"

**Bad Response:**
> "This framework is broken! Nothing works! The docs are useless! Someone fix this NOW!"

**Why first is better:** Professional, provides context, asks for help respectfully

## Community Responsibility

Everyone in the LyraJS community shares responsibility for maintaining a positive environment:

### As a Community Member
- Follow this Code of Conduct
- Report violations when you see them
- Support others who are targeted
- Be an example of positive behavior

### As a Contributor
- Welcome newcomers and help them learn
- Provide constructive code reviews
- Respect maintainers' time and decisions
- Write inclusive documentation and comments

### As a Maintainer
- Enforce this Code of Conduct fairly

- Be transparent about decisions
